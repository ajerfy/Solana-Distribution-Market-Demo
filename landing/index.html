The Problem

Binary markets

Mechanism

How it works

01
𝒩

Submit a distribution

Instead of a binary vote, you submit Normal(μ, σ). μ is where you think the outcome lands. σ is how sure you are.

target: Normal(μ=68, σ=6)
02
𝒟

Collateral from divergence

Your collateral requirement is computed from the KL-divergence between your distribution and the crowd's. High conviction = higher stake.

cost ∝ KL(yours ‖ crowd)
03
✓

Settle on outcomes

At resolution, an oracle posts the realized value. Payout is scored by how close your distribution was to the truth.

payout = f(x_true | μ, σ)

The Insight

Why distributions
unlock better markets

When you can trade uncertainty itself, markets become more efficient — and more honest.

Expressive positions

A trader who thinks 65% but is very uncertain and a trader who thinks 65% with total conviction should not pay the same price. Now they don't.

σ encodes conviction

Truthful incentives

KL-divergence pricing means the only way to minimize your expected cost is to report your true belief — including your uncertainty.

proper scoring rule

Continuous resolution

Settlement isn't binary. The payout function is smooth — a distribution centered 1% off still gets partial credit. No more all-or-nothing cliff.

f(x_true | μ, σ)

Crowd σ as consensus

The aggregate σ across all traders is itself a market signal — a live, on-chain measure of how certain the crowd is. Better than any sentiment index.

crowd σ → VIX-like index
